The SSLC 525 Shift: Why Tulu Must Be Elevated to First Language Status

Presented by The Tuluva Guardian Editorial Committee
Udupi-Tulunadu Regional Headquarters

The restructuring of the Karnataka SSLC marks distribution—reducing the aggregate to 525 and marginalizing the 3rd language—is an administrative maneuver that masks a deeper linguistic crisis. While the Department of Education seeks to alleviate student stress, this Committee contends that the current policy inadvertently creates a path for the systematic erasure of the Tulu language from the academic core.

Performance vs. Opportunity

RTI-backed data confirms a profound disparity in Tuluva education. In specific taluks across the coastal belt, Tulu language students have consistently achieved a 100% pass rate, demonstrating not only high aptitude but a deep-seated desire for linguistic preservation. Conversely, in regions like Kundapura and Brahmavara, enrollment has stagnated at zero due to a lack of institutional infrastructure.

"If a language achieves a 100% success rate in the classroom, it is no longer an 'optional' subject—it is a proven academic asset. The state must reward performance with recognition."

Our Standing Demand

The Tuluva Guardian Editorial Committee formally proposes a two-tiered linguistic restoration plan:

  1. First Language Status: In taluks where Tulu students have maintained a 100% pass rate, the language must be elevated to 1st Language status immediately.
  2. Mandatory Infrastructure: In zones like Kundapura and Brahmavara, the state must provide dedicated Tulu educators to revive the enrollment levels discovered in our recent investigations.

Conclusion

To treat Tulu as a "grade-only" elective is to ignore the historical weight of the Alupa Dynasty and the modern academic excellence of our students. We will not accept the reduction of our heritage to a secondary grade. We demand a curriculum that reflects the linguistic reality of Tulunadu.
